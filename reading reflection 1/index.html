When I started ideating on the Micro Project, I wanted to focus on creating a simple and engaging interaction that felt playful and visually interesting. Reading through Norman’s The Design of Everyday Things, I was able to reflect on my own project and identifying the key concepts he mentions. At the center of the experience is a single square, shown with minimal instruction. This initial uncertainty is intentional allowing the user to discover the process of the interaction with simple cues. Norman emphasizes that good design should be straightforward and present all possible actions to be visible and understandable. In my game, I reverse the flow, and let the simplicity yet vagueness create its own interaction. Although the box does not explicitly instruct the user, it still offers an affordance like the “press me” placed in the center of the box. This minimal addition does not become immediately obvious, but it still exists as a fundamental part of the interaction.
